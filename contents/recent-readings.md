* **The Second Half: Re-thinking AI Evaluation and Utility** (Yao, 2025)
    * *Takeaway:* Yao precisely diagnoses the "Utility Problem" of evaluating agents in static, i.i.d. environments. Reading this deeply resonated with my architectural philosophy for Embodied AI, crystalizing three core system-level convictions:
        * **1. Edge-Side System 2:** Relying on monolithic cloud models for physical interaction is a dead end. To support "slow thinking" in robotics, we need hierarchical architectures where scaling test-time compute unlocks "phase transitions" in physical behaviors.
        * **2. Tools as "Physical APIs":** While language models rely on text for priors, physical agents should derive priors from *tools*. Tools are human-engineered encapsulations of physical laws. Bypassing tabula rasa RL by treating tools as APIs fundamentally solves sample inefficiency.
        * **3. Composable Skills for non-i.i.d. Realities:** The physical world is continuous. To prevent catastrophic forgetting and KV-cache/memory bandwidth explosions during continuous adaptation, the system must shift from global weight updates to dynamically composing frozen **Skill Primitives**—reducing adaptation to a highly efficient, hardware-aware operator scheduling problem (the core motivation behind my `Toolinit`).
